Useful Equipment for making a Banana Loaf Recipe

Making a cake is mostly about ingredients and method but, let's face it, you can't bake a cake without a cake tin. So here we go through the key equipment you might need, or want, to make a cake, particularly our banana loaf, then serve it and store it.

All the items reviewed here are items we own ourselves and use on a regular basis so you know that these are real opinions of real people in a normal kitchen just like yours.

Loaf Tin

When you make a loaf cake, the first investment you need to make is in a loaf tin. Loaf tins tend to come in two main sizes - the 1lb tin (450g) and the 2lb tin (900g). A reasonable size of loaf cake is a 2lb loaf - quite frankly a 1lb loaf is hardly much bigger than your hand and won't serve many people! Our recipe here uses a 2lb loaf tin.

As a rough guide, a 2lb loaf tin is about 21cm long, 11cm wide and 7cm high (8 x 4 x 3 inches approx) and a 1lb loaf tin is 16cm long, 11cm wide and 7cm high (6 x 4 x 3 inches). They do vary quite a bit from brand to brand but this should help you understand what you have got if you have one already. Also beware the difference between internal and external measurements, since a protruding lip will affect measurements.

There are many loaf tins on the market and for the baking on this website we have used a number of different brands of tin. We have narrowed these down to one recommendation, the Paton Calvert Just Cook Non-Stick 2lb Loaf Pan

The Paton Calvert Just Cook Non-Stick 2lb Loaf Pan tin ticks all the boxes for us which include:

  • it’s non-stick
  • it has edges to hold onto - with oven gloves on, you don't want it to slip out of your hands
  • it’s dishwasher safe - though to be honest, its non-stick surface is so good we haven't needed to put it in the dishwasher yet
  • it's a fairly standard size - helpful for buying Loaf Tin Liners (see below)
  • it comes with a 2 year guarantee

The tin is especially impressive given it costs less than half the price of a department store tin yet the quality does not reflect this at all. It is just the perfect tin to use when baking your banana loaf recipe.

Loaf Tin Liners

Lining a loaf tin with greaseproof paper certainly makes the washing up easier. It is also said that the paper also stops the cooling bread or cake from drying out.  But even better than embarking on the origami that is lining your tin with paper, we like to use loaf tin liners.

These are pre-shaped paper liners that are designed to fit standard sized 1lb and 2lb loaf tins. If you're going to make even a few loaf cakes, breads or even meat loafs in your life, we highly recommend getting them.

We have tried a number of brands and have found that the Kitchencraft 2 lb Loaf Tin Liners are the most reasonably priced. They come in packs of 40 and certainly make your life easier. We don’t even grease the loaf tin but simply pop a liner in and fill it with cake mixture. Once the cake is cooked, it is easy to lift the cake out of the tin and there is barely any washing up to do!

Cooling Racks

When you take your cake out of the oven, you want it to cool evenly all over. Leaving it in the tin will cause condensation to quickly build up at the bottom of the tin and the base of the cake will go soggy. So you want it out of the tin as quickly as possible. But putting it on a plate will still cause the underside to get soggy. A cooling rack or tray made from a wire mesh allows air to get to all of your cake and it will then cool evenly without any moisture build-up.

Cooling racks can come in all shapes and sizes. You don’t need a huge one for a banana loaf cake but, if you want to do more general baking, you need to have one that is a reasonable size to fit a dozen fairy cakes on or a couple of sponge cakes. About 40cm x 25cm (16 x 10 inches) is about right.

We particularly like the Tala Non-Stick Cooling Rack. The non-stick (black not silver) nature of it ensures that cakes, bread and biscuits lift off more easily and you don’t find yourself scrubbing stuck-on bits of cake off the wires. It is even dishwasher safe - however, we’ve never needed to put it in the dishwasher as we generally find that cakes lift off so easily that a quick wipe does the job!

For the perfect final step of baking your banana loaf recipe, ensure you cool it properly on a wire rack.

Silicone Spatula

Don’t waste a drop of cake mixture! Make your final cake as big as possible by using a good flexible spatula to scrape out every last bit from your mixing bowl. Silicone spatulas are a great buy as they are heat resistant so have more uses than just for scraping cold mixtures.

We love the colours of the Colourworks Silicone Spatulas, with purple being our favourite choice. However, you can get it in blue, red, green, yellow, orange or pink too. The normal size one is 28cm (11 inches) but you can buy Colourworks Mini Spatulas too, which are 20cm long and great for smaller bowls (and smaller hands!).

Colourworks Spatulas are dishwasher safe and very hygienic as they are made from one continuous piece of silicone. They can withstand very high temperatures and do not melt when they come into contact with a hot pan or tin. They are also great to use on non-stick surfaces as they won’t scratch. Ours has been through the dishwasher over and over again but the colour has not faded and it has not cracked or been scratched.

Cake Tins for Storage

What better way to keep your home baking fresh than by storing it in some colourful cake tins. Over the years we’ve gone through quite a few sets of tins in our house. Pretty metal tins may seem much of a muchness and more about the pattern on the outside than the quality but one thing we often find is that they tarnish easily on the inside, particularly if left on the draining board to dry rather than being dried up immediately. The outsides seem to look fine but the inside silver surface develops ugly black spots that don’t look pretty.

Enter the Tala Retro design cake tins. We’ve had these tins for several years now and not treated them in any special way but, for some reason, their brass-coloured interior surfaces have not developed rust spots in the same way and they look as good as they did the day they were bought. They are also very airtight, keeping cakes fresher for longer.

The three tins are good sizes - the large tin (pale blue with white star design) is 25 cm in diameter and 10 cm deep; the medium tin (pink and white candy stripe) is 22cm diameter and 9 cm deep and the small tin (pale green with white spots) is 17 cm in diameter and 7.5 cm deep. They stack inside each other for maximum space saving and we really like the colours. When thinking about cake tin reviews, we agreed that the Tala Retro design cake tins were the only metal cake tins that we could recommend for cake storage.
