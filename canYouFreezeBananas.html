Can you freeze bananas?

Can you freeze bananas? Absolutely!

If you’ve got a banana that is going brown or even black in the fruit bowl and no-one in the family will touch it - “yuck, Mum, that ones going all horrible!” - then don’t throw it away. Just put it in the freezer! You don’t have to do anything to it - just put it in there (if you like a particularly tidy freezer you could put it in a box or a bag, but there’s no need). When it’s time to make a banana loaf cake, you can just take it out and defrost it and add it to your recipe.

Things to note:-

  • The skin of the banana will go black in the freezer. That’s normal.
  • It takes a couple of hours to defrost the banana at room temperature. You need to defrost it before you can add it to your banana loaf cake mix.
  • When you defrost the banana, the banana will leak and ooze! Don’t just leave it on the side or you’ll have an awful mess. Just pop it on a plate or in a bowl to defrost.
  • When you peel the defrosted banana, the banana flesh will be really mushy. Not very appetising as fresh fruit, but perfect for a good banana loaf cake!
  • We quite often mix and match frozen and fresh bananas in our loaf cakes when we make them but you can make a cake with all frozen too - depends how many you’ve got in the freezer. We find that banana loaf cake is quite forgiving when it comes to of quantity of bananas so just use 3 or 4 bananas and you should be fine. You may just need to adjust the cooking time a bit to compensate if you've got a particularly wet mixture. See our article Is my Banana Loaf cooked? for more help.
