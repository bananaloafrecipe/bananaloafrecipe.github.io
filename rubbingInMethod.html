The Rubbing-in Method

When you make a cake, there are many different ways to combine the ingredients. One of those is known as the "rubbing-in method".  Quite simply, it is the act of using your fingers to combine hard fat (butter, margarine or lard) and flour by rubbing them together.

One of the best reasons for using the rubbing-in method is that the hard work is done by our own hands so no machine or gadget is needed. And it is not difficult - even a child can do it!  But don't hang about - it should be done quickly and lightly, introducing a bit of air where possible and without melting the fat.

By the way, you may be familiar with this method if you have ever made home-made crumble for a fruit crumble pudding or home-made pastry - you may just not have known it had a name!

Here is our step-by-step guide.

  1. Weigh your flour and put into a roomy mixing bowl. You need to get your hands in the bowl so give yourself a decant-sized bowl so you have space to work.
  2. Weigh your fat (butter or margarine). It doesn't have to be room temperature like with other cakes - straight from the fridge is actually better as the fat won't melt as you rub.
  3. Put the fat into the bowl with the flour.
  4. Start with a table knife and chop the fat into small pieces.
  5. Once the fat is well chopped, it's time to get your hands dirty! (Ensure you have clean hands).
  6. Using both hands, pick up handfuls of fat and flour and rub them together with your fingertips. The fat and flour will combine into what look a bit like breadcrumbs. Try and lift up the fat and flour as you do it so you introduce air into the mixture  - do the rubbing above not in the mixture. Use the tips of your fingers not your whole hands.
  7. Keep going at this until all the lumps of fat have disappeared and you are left with a whole bowl of breadcrumb-like particles.
  8. This should take no more than 5 minutes (once you've got the hang of it!)
  9. You are ready to move on to the next stage of your recipe.
